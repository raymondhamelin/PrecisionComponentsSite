About

The work is grounded in something most advisory firms can't claim.

Precision Components exists because of a specific kind of experience — one earned in environments where performance is non-negotiable, alignment is a survival requirement, and execution failures have real consequences. That foundation shapes everything about how the firm operates today.

Nick Lavery · Founder

01 — The foundation

Nick Lavery.

Nick is an active-duty Special Forces Chief Warrant Officer — and the first above-the-knee amputee in military history to return to sustained combat operations on a Special Forces team. That distinction isn't a footnote. It's the clearest possible proof of what this work is actually built on: the capacity to perform at the highest level when the conditions are hardest.

Nick is also the bestselling author of Objective Secure, in which he maps the mental and operational frameworks that allowed him to return to the fight — and shows how those same frameworks apply inside any organization serious about performance.

He founded Precision Components with a specific belief: that the principles governing how elite teams align, make decisions, and execute under pressure aren't unique to military environments. They transfer. And when applied with discipline inside complex organizations, they change how those organizations perform.

02 — The evolutionFrom capability to firm

What it's become.

Precision Components began as a single capability and has grown into something more substantial — a firm with the structure, the team, and the operating model to deliver this work at scale.

That evolution was intentional. The work PC does — embedded advisory, experiential engagements, implementation that holds — requires more than one person can sustain across multiple organizations simultaneously. It requires a team built around the work itself, with the depth to stay inside an engagement as it extends and the continuity to make improvement durable.

Today, Precision Components operates with an internal team that runs the firm and a field team of specialists deployed into engagements based on fit — ensuring the right people are in the room for the right work, every time.
